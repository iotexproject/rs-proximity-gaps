## The α formula and the Pell-c-equation

For M = {0, 2, 4, 5, 7}, the symbolic minor (0, 2, 5) gives α as a rational
function of (c, ω):
$$\alpha = -B(c, \omega) / A(c, \omega)$$
where (in the symbolic output of `issue419_path_A_alpha_parametrization.py`):
- $A = c_2(\omega^5 + 2\omega^4 - 2\omega^2 - \omega) + c_3(-\omega^7 + \omega^6 + \omega^5 - \omega^3 - \omega^2 + \omega) + c_6(-\omega^6 + \omega^5 + \omega^4 - \omega^2 - \omega + 1) + c_7(\omega^6 + 2\omega^5 - 2\omega^3 - \omega^2)$
- $B = c_4(\omega^6 - \omega^4 - \omega^2 + 1) + c_5(-\omega^7 + \omega^5 + \omega^3 - \omega)$

For α to satisfy the Pell equation $\alpha^2 - 2\alpha - 1 = 0$, we need:
$$\boxed{B^2 + 2AB - A^2 \equiv 0 \pmod p.}$$

- α = -10 · 16⁻¹ = -10 · 18 = -180 ≡ 25 (mod 41) ✓
- Pell-c-eq: B² + 2AB − A² = 100 + 320 − 256 = 164 = 4·41 ≡ 0 ✓

## Structural-proof skeleton

The failure variety per (S, M) is cut out by:
1. **Kernel constraint** ($s \in S$): $\sum_{r \in [2,8)} c_r \omega^{rs} = 0$ — 4 linear F_p-equations on $(c_2,...,c_7)$, leaving a **2-dim kernel** $K_S$.
2. **α-only constraint** (degenerate minor (0, 2, 4)):
   $A_0(c, \omega) := (c_2 + c_6) + \omega^2 (c_3 + c_7) = 0$ — 1 linear equation
   in $K_S$, cutting to **1-dim** $K_S'$.
3. **Pell-c-equation** (the resultant of α-uniqueness conditions):
   $B^2 + 2AB - A^2 = 0$ — degree-2 in c, cutting $K_S'$ to **0-dim**.

The 0-dim variety has at most 2 projective F_p-rational points per (S, M) —
one per Pell root.

**To prove $K_2 \leq 1$ unconditional**, it suffices to show:

> **Claim (structural)**: The Pell-c-equation $B^2 + 2AB - A^2 = 0$ is
> identically equivalent to **a polynomial in the ideal generated by the
> kernel constraints + the α-only constraint $A_0 = 0$**, modulo $\omega^8 - 1$.

If true, then the failure variety per (S, M) has codimension at least 2 in
$K_S$ (kernel + A_0 + Pell-c-eq), reducing to 0-dim, with at most 2 projective
F_p-points. Since each projective point corresponds to a unique Pell root α,
**K_2 ≤ 2 per (S, M)**.

To get **K_2 ≤ 1 per (S, c-projective)**, we further note: scaling c → λc
preserves the projective direction and preserves α (since α = -B/A is
homogeneous of degree 0 in c). So per projective direction, exactly one α
value (one Pell root). Hence K_2 ≤ 1 per (S, c-projective).

## Why the Pell equation appears: Galois interpretation

The construction at base scale (8, 2) has:
- u-side support: $\{4, 5\} \subset \mathbb{Z}/8$
- v-side support: $\{2, 3, 6, 7\} \subset \mathbb{Z}/8$

These supports are **not invariant** under any non-trivial element of the
Galois group $\mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_8)/\mathbb{Q}) = (\mathbb{Z}/8)^*
= \{1, 3, 5, 7\}$. Hence the failure α is not Galois-invariant in the naive
sense.

However: the failure variety is defined over $\mathbb{Q}[\zeta_8]$ (after
clearing denominators in the resultant elimination). The α-projection of the
failure variety is a 0-dim subscheme of $\mathbb{A}^1_{\mathbb{Q}[\zeta_8]}$.
Empirically this subscheme has **2 points**, satisfying $\alpha^2 - 2\alpha
- 1 = 0$ — the minimal polynomial of $1 + \sqrt 2$ over $\mathbb{Q}$.

The square root of 2 arises naturally in $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_8)$ via
$\zeta_8 + \zeta_8^{-1} = 2 \cos(\pi/4) = \sqrt 2$. So $\sqrt 2 \in
\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_8)$ and the Pell roots $1 \pm \sqrt 2$ live in this
cyclotomic field. The reduction mod p (with $8 | p-1$) sends $\sqrt 2$
to a square root of 2 in $\mathbb{F}_p$ (which always exists since
$p \equiv 1 \mod 8$ implies $2$ is a QR mod p).

This is the cleanest "Galois explanation" — not orbit-decomposition but
**field-theoretic**: $\sqrt 2 \in \mathbb{Q}(\zeta_8)$ via the Gauss-sum
identity $\zeta_8 + \zeta_8^{-1} = \sqrt 2$, and the failure α is a polynomial
expression in $\zeta_8$ that lands in $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt 2)$.
