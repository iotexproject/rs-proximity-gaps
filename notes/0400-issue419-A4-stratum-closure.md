## 2.  Reduction of (II) to a 4-root disagreement test

Write $X(y) := (P + y^{r/2}) / G_A(y)$ (well-defined polynomial since
$G_A \mid P + y^{r/2}$ by (D-P)).  Write $Y(y) := Q(y) / G_A(y) = \lambda \cdot Y_0(y) / G_A(y)$.

After dividing (II) [= $(P + y^{r/2}) G_o \equiv Q G_e \pmod{G_*}$] through
by $G_A$ and using $G_*(y) = G_A(y) \cdot H(y)$ where $H(y) := G_e(y)^2 - y G_o(y)^2$,
the reduced condition is:
$$
X(y) G_o(y) \equiv \lambda \cdot \tilde Y_0(y) G_e(y) \pmod{H(y)},
\qquad \tilde Y_0 := Y_0 / G_A
$$
(in $\mathbb{F}_q[y]/H(y)$).

**Roots of $H(y)$.**  $H(y) = G_e^2 - y G_o^2 = g_B(t) g_B(-t) / 1$ in $t$-form,
which has roots at $t = \omega^b$ for $b \in B \cup (B + n/2)$.  In $y = t^2$,
this gives 4 distinct values $\eta_c := \zeta^c$ for $c \in \{b \bmod 8 : b \in B\}$
(8th roots of unity, since $\omega^{2 \cdot (b+8)} = \omega^{2b}$).

**Key identity at each root $\eta_c$.**  Since $g_B(\omega^b) = 0$ for the
unique $b \in B$ with $b \bmod 8 = c$:
$$
G_e(\eta_c) + \omega^b G_o(\eta_c) = 0
\;\Longrightarrow\;
G_e(\eta_c) = -\omega^b G_o(\eta_c).
$$
Substituting into (II)-mod-$H$:
$$

$$
so (assuming $G_o(\eta_c) \ne 0$):
$$
\boxed{\;\lambda \;=\; \frac{-X(\eta_c)}{\omega^{b_c} \tilde Y_0(\eta_c)}\;}
\qquad (\text{derived } \lambda \text{ at root } \eta_c).

## 3.  Why disagreement is generic — structural sketch

The derived $\lambda$ values are:
$$
\lambda_c = \frac{-X(\zeta^c)}{\omega^{b_c} \tilde Y_0(\zeta^c)},
\qquad c \in \{b \bmod 8 : b \in B\}, \;|B| = 4.
$$

For $\lambda_{c_1} = \lambda_{c_2}$:
$$
X(\zeta^{c_1}) \cdot \omega^{b_{c_2}} \tilde Y_0(\zeta^{c_2}) = X(\zeta^{c_2}) \cdot \omega^{b_{c_1}} \tilde Y_0(\zeta^{c_1}).
$$

Both sides are polynomial expressions in $(\zeta_1, \zeta_2, \omega)$.  The
no-full constraint on $S$ (which forces $b_1, b_2, b_3, b_4 \in B$ to span
4 distinct mod-8 residues, with at most 3 in any quadrant of $S$) yields
generic non-equality.

A clean closed-form proof requires unwinding the symmetric polynomials
$s_{m-1}(\zeta_1, \zeta_2)$, the residue computations $\zeta_i^{r/2}$ in
8th-root algebra, and showing the resultant-type expression
$\mathrm{LHS} - \mathrm{RHS}$ is nonzero modulo the ideal generated by
no-full constraints.

This closed-form proof is the next concrete sub-task; the empirical
prime-uniform confirmation already establishes the result *as-stated* for
