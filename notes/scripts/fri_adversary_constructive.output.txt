 • Adversarial accept rate ≈ (w+1)/|F|; random baseline is ~10-100×

 • This DEMONSTRATES the codim-2(c-1) structure: V_bad is a small but
   adversarially-attainable subvariety; the adversary can deterministically
   construct witnesses (no luck needed) and force the verifier accept rate
   to M/|F| = (w+1)/|F| = Θ(1) at small p, Θ(1/|F|^{c-1}) at deployment p.

 Reviewer C#3's question — 'is V_bad the right event?' — answered:
   YES, V_bad is exactly the locus an adversarial prover can hit by
   choosing (s_1, s_2) ∈ V_S* × V_S*. Theorem 3.1's codim 2(c-1) is the
   tight measure of how small that locus is.
